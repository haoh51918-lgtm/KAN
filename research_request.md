## Research intent

Investigate a MIRAGE-KAN family of systems in which typed KAN-based miners produce fixed executable factor programs and, if evidence supports it, a Miner-Factor controller coordinates search. `KAN_Alpha_PR.md` is the original idea draft: it supplies hypotheses and design options, but no section is binding merely because it says “must”, “locked”, or “sole authority”. Claims and architecture may contract or change when evidence warrants it.

## User requirements

1. Advance the real scientific questions before adding governance, audit, or reporting machinery.
2. Build small complete chains early; refine only seams that evidence identifies as limiting.
3. Preserve causal/type/data-opening correctness, but do not confuse reversible software work with irreversible scientific access.
4. Judge candidate libraries primarily through matched cost-aware QuantaAlpha evidence. Factor count, runtime, training rounds, and paperwork are diagnostics.
5. Treat implementation failures as bugs: reproduce, red-test, fix, and rerun the same reversible path. Do not create a successor protocol unless scientific semantics change.
6. Keep deliverables under this workspace and external repositories read-only.
7. Send Feishu or other external reports only when the current user explicitly requests or authorizes that transmission.
