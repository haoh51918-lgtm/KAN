## What the sequence teaches

v2, v4, v5, v6, v7, and v8 were primarily failures at serialization, data selection, lineage comparison, sparse indexing, hashing, or invocation identity seams. Creating a new scientific successor after each ordinary software defect multiplied locks, launchers, receipts, and paths, which created more seams than it removed. Future implementation failures stay in the software debugging loop unless scientific semantics change.

The seven-factor v3/v6 result also indicates that the present E3 space may be the scientific bottleneck. The next response is exact enumeration and operator-space diagnosis, not a ninth rerun of the same chain.

## Attempt policy

Budgets belong to the experiment that preregistered them. There is no global default attempt quota and no automatic successor entitlement. Reversible debugging is bounded by reproducing and fixing the concrete defect; repeated same-seam failures trigger simplification or redesign.
