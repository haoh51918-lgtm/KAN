# Principal decision: graph cooperative mining is a core post-v6 component

- **Date**: 2026-07-17 UTC
- **Decision maker**: principal
- **Status**: authorized
- **Effective scope**: successor protocols and milestones after the already locked v6 transaction

## Decision

The graph component is unlocked and promoted from a deferred or ablation-only
option to a core component of the cooperative factor-mining mainline. It may be
designed and implemented in parallel with exact-space baselines, operator-space
expansion, and library-selection work.

The graph is initially responsible for coordinating miners through task
assignment, diversity repulsion, structured variation, and budget allocation.
Published factors must remain fixed, independently executable typed ASTs; the
graph may control search but may not become a hidden inference-time dependency.

## Evidence boundary

Unlocking development does not pre-approve a scientific superiority claim.
Any graph advantage remains subject to a common-interface Gate B comparison of
random, flat, bandit, and graph controllers under matched data, search space,
budget, selection, and backtest rules. If graph does not outperform simpler
controllers, the claim must contract to cooperative closed-loop mining and the
graph must be reported as one controller implementation.

## v6 non-retroactivity

v6 had already written immutable locks and consumed its mining entitlement.
Its `graph_unlock_allowed: false` value remains historically and scientifically
correct for that frozen transaction. No v6 file, lock, opening, or result is
rewritten. Successor configuration will set the graph gate according to this
decision after the Living Manual authority decision is resolved.
