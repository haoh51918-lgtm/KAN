# Principal decision: adopt review guidance and a living research manual

- **Date**: 2026-07-17 UTC
- **Decision maker**: principal
- **Status**: authorized
- **Source feedback**: `Review-from-claude.md`, review #1

## Authority hierarchy

1. `KAN_Alpha_PR.md` remains the original idea draft and historical baseline.
   It is not an execution authority or a permanently current operations manual.
2. `docs/research/MIRAGE_KAN_LIVING_MANUAL.md` is the maintained authority for
   the current research route, execution priorities, and behavioral constraints.
3. `Review-from-claude.md` is an independent key guidance document. It remains
   append-only feedback rather than an experiment result or preregistration.
4. Principal decision logs authorize material changes to the Living Manual or
   departures from the original proposal.
5. A frozen protocol, lock, opening, or published result is never changed
   retroactively by a later manual or review update.

## Decisions adopted

The principal approved all seven requested decisions:

1. Use the original proposal plus a continuously maintained Living Manual.
2. Complete v6 as the clean baseline for the existing small strict space, then
   stop treating local tuning of that space as the main growth route.
3. Unlock graph cooperative mining as a core component while preserving the
   random/flat/bandit/graph Gate B comparison.
4. Seal 2026-01 through 2026-06 as unseen confirmation data until the successor
   framework and analysis protocol are frozen.
5. Restrict quarantined v5 metrics to risk registration and experiment-coverage
   design; forbid their use for factor choice, tuning, threshold changes, or
   scientific claims.
6. Adopt the M-A through M-E roadmap and proposed parallel ordering.
7. After v6, initialize local Git, move all data-independent checks and a full
   synthetic dress rehearsal before opening consumption, and implement a
   verify-and-rebind mechanism for deterministic cross-protocol topology reuse.
   Off-host backup execution remains pending a destination and retention policy.

## Adaptive execution rule

Long-horizon research is expected to encounter scientific and engineering
failures. The agent must continuously diagnose and update the Living Manual,
plans, risk register, and decision requests. Adaptation is required, but it may
not silently mutate a frozen experiment, consume unseen confirmation data, or
turn quarantined observations into scientific evidence.
